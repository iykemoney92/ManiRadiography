Planes of the body

Three planes of the body are used extensively for descriptions of positioning both in plain-film imaging and in cross-sectional imaging techniques. The planes described are mutually at rightangles to each other:

  • • Median sagittal plane: divides the body into right and left halves. Any plane that is parallel to this but divides the body into unequal right and left portions is known simply as a sagittal plane or parasagittal plane.
  • • Coronal plane: divides the body into an anterior part and a posterior part.
  • • Transverse or axial plane: divides the body into a superior part and an inferior part.

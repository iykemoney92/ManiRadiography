Thoracic Spine(Lateral)

Centering point:The central ray should be at right-angles to the long axis of the thoracic vertebrae. This may require a caudal angulation.
Centre 5 cm anterior to the spinous process of T6/7. This is usually found just below the inferior angle of the scapula (assuming the arms are raised), which is easily palpable

Cassette Size:35cm x 43cm (17 x 14ins) larger patient 30cm x 35cm (14 x 14ins) smaller patient Portrait

Exposure Factors:75kVp on 40MaS

FFD:120cm

Bucky/Grid:Moving or Stationary Grid

Filter:No

Collimation:Collimate tightly to the spine

Pathologies:Fractures, spondylolisthesis, bone lesions, osteoporosis, foreign bodies

Position of patient and cassette

  • Usually undertaken with the patient in the lateral decubitus position on the X-ray table, although this projection can also be performed erect.

  • The arms should be raised well above the head
  • The head can be supported with a pillow, and pads may be placed between the knees for the patient’s comfort
  • The upper edge of the cassette should be at least 40 cm in length and should be positioned 3–4 cm above the spinous process of C7
